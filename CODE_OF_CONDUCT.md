# Code of conduct

## Our standard

This project is a place to build something useful together. Everyone taking part is
expected to be respectful and constructive. That means welcoming newcomers, giving
feedback on the work rather than the person, accepting that people have different levels
of experience, and assuming good faith.

The following are not acceptable: harassment of any kind, personal or political attacks,
demeaning or discriminatory comments, publishing someone's private information, and
sustained disruption of discussion.

## Scope

This applies in all project spaces, the issue tracker, pull requests, and discussions,
and when someone represents the project in public.

## Enforcement

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, report it privately to the
maintainers through GitHub's private vulnerability reporting on this repository, or by a
direct message to the repository owner. Reports are handled confidentially. The
maintainers may warn, remove content, or block a participant whose behavior they judge
inappropriate, and will explain the decision to the person reporting.
